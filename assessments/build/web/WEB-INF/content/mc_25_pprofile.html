What exactly is Value Intelligence©

  • Simply, it is the knowledge and understanding of one's own private, personal, and individual beliefs about what is most important to them within a defined environment.
  • Your values are your belief system about what you classify as right or wrong, good or bad.
  • Our values are the things we all fundamentally need to move towards, in an understanding of the congruent, or personal wholeness in the unity of self, which comes from the sense that we are fulfilling our very values, by our present behaviour within a defined environment.
  • Our values govern our entire lifestyle.
  • They also determine how we would respond to any given experience at any given time.

Values are much like the Central Processing Unit in a computer:

  • You can install any program or information you like, but whether the computer accepts the program or information or whether it uses it or not, all depends on how the CPU was programmed by the manufacturer.
  • Values are the Central Processing Unit of Judgment.
  • Almost all of us have a bottom line - things that are more important to us, above anything else.
  • For some people it is prestige, for others it is creativity.
  • For others it will be important to be in the limelight all the time, including in their working environment, in order to fulfil their value of prestige.
  • For another it will be much more important to be creative in their environment - prestige might not be an issue to them.
  • When one understand one's true values, you will understand why individuals do certain things, and why people do not enjoy the same things.
  • Values are the key to unlock the door to a lifestyle of peace and prosperity.
  • This understanding and knowledge of personal values according to importance, combined with clearly defined environmental values is called Value Intelligence ©.

To obtain a harmonious and ultimate lifestyle, we need the intelligence necessary to identify the constructs that are most important to us, by specifically knowing and understanding our hierarchy of personal values within a defined environment.
